Meanwhile, in a certain nice little split-level midwestern nestern...

The telephone rings.

ALBERT: Rose, would you get that? I'm busy trying to grade papers while cooking, without cooking the papers...

The telephone rings again.

ALBERT: Well, I suppose I could try to answer it and risk burning the tortillas. But I've got to grade these papers by tomorrow and it's already midnight!

ALBERT: Bah, where on earth is she? This is probably an important call...

Albert answers the phone.

ALBERT: Albert J. Peterson, speaking. Hello? Hello? I'm sorry who is this? I can barely hear you over all the terrifying gurgles and groans and screams...hello?

Albert gently places the phone back on the receiver.

ALBERT: Huh. How rude.

ALBERT: If it's important they'll call back. Papers about appropriately submissive gender roles won't grade themselves!

The sounds of crashing thunder and a torrential downpour invade the house. Suddenly, a shotgun blast rings out and the front door swings open. A drenched Rosie rushes in and slams it shut.

ROSIE: They're everywhere, Albert - everywhere!

Albert notices that Rosie is brandishing a shotgun...

ALBERT: Rosie, I thought you were upstairs! Who's everywhere? What are you talking about? And please watch where you point that thing...

ROSIE: Albert, you said you already set up the scarecrow - the birds are eating all of the corn! They're not even afraid of the gun anymore. Thank heavens this rain is driving them away...

ALBERT: The scarecrow! Oh Rosie, I'm sorry - I forgot. I'll set it up first thing in the morning.

Another sudden thunder crash, and the house goes dark.

ALBERT: The storm must have knocked out the power. The flashlights are in the basement, I'll go get them.

Albert's footsteps recede down the creaky stairs.

One minute passes.

Two minutes pass.

Three minutes now.

ROSIE: Al...Albert? Hello?

The stairs creak once more, one step at a time.

ROSIE: Albert? Did you find the flashlight?

VOICE IN THE DARKNESS: GRRaaaackagggraraaha...

ROSIE: That's not funny Albert - turn the light on!

VOICE IN THE DARKNESS: Zzzrrhgrak...

HOODED FIGURE CLOTHED IN GOLD SEQUINS: Alllllbeeeeeert!

ROSIE: Get back from the window now Albert!

Albert doesn't move, he just stares at the figure in the window.

The figure reaches for Albert with bloody hands and groans again...

HOODED FIGURE: ALLLLLLLLLBEEERRRRRT!

ROSIE: For the love of God don't just stand there!

He takes an incredulous step toward the window frame.

ALBERT: Is...is that...

The figure lunges for Albert and tumbles into the kitchen.

HOODED FIGURE: AAAAAAAALLLLLLLBEEERRRRRRT!

ROSIE: (Muttering) Do I have to do everything myself?

She grabs a scalding hot skillet from the nearby stove, flings the charred tortilla out the window, and brandishes the skillet over her head. Meanwhile, Albert inches closer to the writhing figure on the ground.

Wiry, clawlike hands seize Albert's wrists - his weapons clatter to the floor.

HOODED FIGURE: AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAALLLLLLLBEEERRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRRT!

Rosie must act now, but Albert is in the way! Her eyes dart feverishly between her husband and the dark invader.

ROSIE: Sorry honey, you'll thank me for this later.

CLANG! Albert groans and drops like a stone.

HOODED FIGURE: GRRRRRRRRRRR!

It leaps at Rose. She ducks, spins and slams the door as the monster sails passed her into the bathroom.

ALBERT: Wha....?

ROSIE: We'll finish it off later - there's no time.

She pulls Albert to his feet.

ALBERT: You...hit me?

ROSIE: NO TIME!

Rosie darts out the door.

Albert follows unsteadily, probing the lump on his head with the side of his handgun.

Growls and crashes resound ominously from behind the closed bathroom door.

CLANG! Rose knocks the creature back four feet against the door. It's sequined hood flies up.

Albert's eyes widen with recognition.

ALBERT: Ma? What on earth are you doing outside in that ridiculous outfit?

Mae steps into the light rubbing a lump on her forehead.

MAE: I'm here to save you Albert!

ALBERT: Save me from...?

Mae glares at Rose. Rose glares right back.

Rose indicates the skillet in her hand with a threatening glance.

Mae winces and returns her attention to Albert.

MAE: You mean besides her?

ALBERT: Not now Ma, we've been over this...

MAE: Fine. Still, you have to come with me. I found somewhere safe, and she's not invited.

ALBERT: Safe?

ROSIE: Ignore her Albert, I know where she's going and you'll both end up worse than dead...

MAE: You're one to talk! If you knew what was good for you'd be out of the city by now.

ROSIE: Your wrong Mae, the city is the only safe place right n...

ROSIE: Quick! Nail those chairs to the door frame! Wait, what was that...?
